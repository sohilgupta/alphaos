# Theme: AI Supply Chain Broadening

## Core Thesis
AI infrastructure leadership is broadening structurally beyond GPUs. The capex supercycle now pulls on a full supplier stack: IP, fabs, memory, packaging, equipment, networking, servers, power systems and power electronics.

This extends the [[AI-Infrastructure-Capex-Supercycle]] thesis: as inference demand becomes always-on, bottlenecks migrate outward from accelerator supply into the supporting physical and semiconductor infrastructure.

### Equipment Layer Correction
Equipment companies ($KLAC, $LRCX, $ASML, $KEYS) supply to **foundries** ($TSM, $INTC), not directly to NVDA. The exposure is second-order: GPU demand → fab utilization → equipment spend. Important for sizing position / conviction relative to direct suppliers.

## Risks / Watchpoints
- The map mixes secular winners with cyclical suppliers; not every company captures the same margin or duration.
- Equipment names are second-order (foundry utilization), not direct AI bottlenecks.
- Margin volatility remains a risk for server OEMs and legacy cyclicals.
- Monitor for supply chain deconcentration away from TSM as geopolitical risk grows.
